# Regulation 3(a) / Companies Act 2006 amendments — National security defences for company name requirements

## Obligation

**Who:** Companies and financial market participants subject to [[Companies Act 2006]] company name rules
**What:** National security-related defences and exemptions to new company name requirements are brought into force. Firms must understand these defences when assessing counterparty or investee company compliance with naming obligations.
**Where:** Corporate governance, counterparty due diligence, and legal compliance frameworks
**Deadline:** 15 January 2024
**Materiality:** medium

## Legal Basis

> [[Regulation 3(a)]] to (e) bring into force various provisions inserted into the [[Companies Act 2006]] (c. 46) by the Act which establish national security-related defences, exemptions and exceptions to new requirements concerning company names, identity verification requirements and false statement offences.
