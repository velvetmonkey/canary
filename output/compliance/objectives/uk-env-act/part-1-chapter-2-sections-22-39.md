# Part 1, Chapter 2 (Sections 22–39) — OEP Enforcement of Environmental Law Against Public Authorities

## Obligation

**Who:** Office for Environmental Protection (OEP); public authorities subject to environmental law
**What:** The OEP has statutory powers to scrutinise, investigate, and take enforcement action (including environmental review before the High Court) against public authorities that fail to comply with environmental law. Public authorities must cooperate with OEP information notices and decision notices. This creates a new independent enforcement body overseeing environmental compliance.
**Where:** OEP investigations, information notices, decision notices, and environmental reviews before the High Court
**Materiality:** medium

## Legal Basis

> "the court" means—(a)in relation to an environmental review arising under the law of England and Wales or Northern Ireland, the High Court
