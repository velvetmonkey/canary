# Part 6 (Section 98) / Schedule 14, Schedule 7A paragraph 9 — 30-Year Habitat Maintenance Obligation Post-Development

## Obligation

**Who:** Developers and landowners delivering biodiversity net gain in England
**What:** Any habitat enhancement delivered as part of a biodiversity gain plan (onsite or offsite) must be secured and maintained for a minimum of 30 years after development is completed. This requires developers to put in place long-term legal agreements (e.g. conservation covenants or planning obligations) to guarantee habitat outcomes.
**Where:** Legal agreements registered against land; biodiversity gain plans approved by planning authorities
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> be maintained for at least 30 years after the development is completed.
