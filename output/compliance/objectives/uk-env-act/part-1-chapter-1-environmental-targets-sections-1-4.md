# Part 1, Chapter 1 — Environmental Targets (Sections 1–4) — Legally binding environmental targets must be set and met

## Obligation

**Who:** Secretary of State; by extension, public authorities and regulated entities whose activities bear on target metrics
**What:** The Secretary of State must set long-term, legally binding environmental targets (covering air quality, water, biodiversity, resource efficiency and waste) and ensure they are met by the specified date and standard. Asset managers with UK-listed or UK-operating investee companies must monitor whether those companies' activities are aligned with or in breach of these targets, as target performance feeds directly into [[SFDR]] principal adverse impact (PAI) and Taxonomy assessments.
**Where:** Targets must be laid before Parliament; compliance is monitored by the OEP and reported publicly
**Deadline:** First targets laid before Parliament on or before 31 October 2022
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> It is the duty of the Secretary of State to ensure that— would significantly improve the natural environment in England.
