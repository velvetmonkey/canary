# Part 7 — Conservation Covenants (Sections 117–138) — Conservation covenants bind land to long-term conservation obligations

## Obligation

**Who:** Landowners entering conservation covenants; responsible bodies (charities, public authorities, local authorities)
**What:** Landowners may enter legally binding conservation covenants with responsible bodies, creating perpetual or long-term obligations to conserve the natural environment, biodiversity or heritage of land. These covenants run with the land and are enforceable against successors. Asset managers investing in real assets or natural capital strategies under [[Article 9]] funds should assess whether target assets carry conservation covenant obligations and whether those obligations support or constrain sustainable investment objectives.
**Where:** Registered against the title to land; enforceable by the responsible body through the courts or Upper Tribunal
**Materiality:** low

## Legal Basis

> a reference to conserving something includes a reference to protecting, restoring or enhancing it
