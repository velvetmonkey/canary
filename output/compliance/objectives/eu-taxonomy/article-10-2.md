# Article 10(2) — Transitional activities: conditions for climate change mitigation qualification

## Obligation

**Who:** Financial market participants and undertakings assessing Taxonomy alignment for transitional economic activities
**What:** For economic activities with no technologically and economically feasible low-carbon alternative, ensure the activity qualifies as substantially contributing to climate change mitigation only if it: (a) has GHG emissions at best-in-class sector level; (b) does not hamper low-carbon alternatives; and (c) does not create carbon-intensive asset lock-in.
**Where:** Applied in investment selection and Taxonomy alignment assessment processes
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> an economic activity for which there is no technologically and economically feasible low-carbon alternative shall qualify as contributing substantially to climate change mitigation where it supports the transition to a climate-neutral economy consistent with a pathway to limit the temperature increase to 1,5 0C above pre-industrial levels, including by phasing out greenhouse gas emissions, in particular emissions from solid fossil fuels.
