# Article 10(1) and 10(2) — Substantial contribution criteria for climate change mitigation

## Obligation

**Who:** Financial market participants and undertakings assessing Taxonomy alignment for climate change mitigation
**What:** Assess whether an economic activity substantially contributes to climate change mitigation by contributing to GHG stabilisation through one of the listed pathways (renewable energy, energy efficiency, clean mobility, etc.), or — for activities without low-carbon alternatives — by meeting best-in-class GHG performance, not hampering low-carbon alternatives, and not creating carbon lock-in.
**Where:** Applied in investment analysis and Taxonomy alignment assessments
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> An economic activity shall qualify as contributing substantially to climate change mitigation where that activity contributes substantially to the stabilisation of greenhouse gas concentrations in the atmosphere at a level which prevents dangerous anthropogenic interference with the climate system consistent with the long-term temperature goal of the Paris Agreement through the avoidance or reduction of greenhouse gas emissions or the increase of greenhouse gas removals.
