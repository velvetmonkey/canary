# Article 17 — Do No Significant Harm (DNSH) assessment across all six objectives

## Obligation

**Who:** Financial market participants and undertakings assessing Taxonomy alignment
**What:** Assess whether an economic activity significantly harms any of the six environmental objectives, taking into account the life cycle of products and services. Harm includes significant GHG emissions, increased climate vulnerability, deterioration of water bodies, material inefficiencies, increased waste, significant pollution increases, or damage to biodiversity and ecosystems.
**Where:** Applied in investment due diligence and product classification processes
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> For the purposes of point (b) of [[Article 3]], taking into account the life cycle of the products and services provided by an economic activity, including evidence from existing life-cycle assessments, that economic activity shall be considered to significantly harm: (a) climate change mitigation, where that activity leads to significant greenhouse gas emissions;
