# Article 17(1) — Do No Significant Harm (DNSH) assessment across all six objectives

## Obligation

**Who:** Financial market participants and undertakings assessing taxonomy alignment
**What:** Assess whether an economic activity significantly harms any of the six environmental objectives, taking into account the life cycle of products and services. Significant harm includes: significant GHG emissions (climate mitigation), increased adverse climate impact (adaptation), deterioration of water bodies, significant material inefficiencies or waste increases (circular economy), significant increase in pollutant emissions, or significant detriment to ecosystems and biodiversity.
**Where:** Applied in investment due diligence and taxonomy alignment assessments
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> For the purposes of point (b) of Article 3, taking into account the life cycle of the products and services provided by an economic activity, including evidence from existing life-cycle assessments, that economic activity shall be considered to significantly harm: (a) climate change mitigation, where that activity leads to significant greenhouse gas emissions.
