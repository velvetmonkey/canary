# Article 16 — Enabling activities: conditions for Taxonomy qualification

## Obligation

**Who:** Financial market participants and undertakings assessing Taxonomy alignment for enabling activities
**What:** An enabling activity (one that directly enables other activities to make a substantial environmental contribution) qualifies as Taxonomy-aligned only if it: (a) does not create asset lock-in undermining long-term environmental goals; and (b) has a substantial positive environmental impact on a life-cycle basis.
**Where:** Applied in investment selection and Taxonomy alignment assessment processes
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> An economic activity shall qualify as contributing substantially to one or more of the environmental objectives set out in [[Article 9]] by directly enabling other activities to make a substantial contribution to one or more of those objectives, provided that such economic activity: (a) does not lead to a lock-in of assets that undermine long-term environmental goals, considering the economic lifetime of those assets; and (b) has a substantial positive environmental impact, on the basis of life-cycle considerations.
