# Section 455 — FMC reporting entities must keep proper accounting records

## Obligation

**Who:** Every FMC reporting entity (including issuers of regulated products, licensed market service providers, listed issuers, registered banks, licensed insurers, credit unions, building societies, and managers of registered schemes)
**What:** FMC reporting entities must keep accounting records at all times that correctly record transactions, enable preparation of GAAP-compliant financial statements, and enable proper audit. They must also establish and maintain a satisfactory system of control over accounting records. Contravention can give rise to civil liability including pecuniary penalties up to NZD 5 million.
**Where:** Maintained at the registered office or another place directors think fit; if kept overseas, summary documents must be kept in New Zealand
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> Every FMC reporting entity must ensure that there are kept at all times accounting records that— (a) correctly record the transactions of the FMC reporting entity; and (b) in the case of a manager of a registered scheme, correctly record the transactions of the scheme; and (c) will enable the FMC reporting entity to ensure that the financial statements of the FMC reporting entity and of any such scheme comply with generally accepted accounting practice and any prescribed requirements; and (d) will enable the financial statements of the FMC reporting entity and of any such scheme to be readily and properly audited.
