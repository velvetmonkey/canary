# Schedule 11, Part 5 (Central Counterparties special resolution regime) — CCPs must comply with Bank of England resolution powers

## Obligation

**Who:** Recognised central counterparties (CCPs) and their clearing members, shareholders, and creditors
**What:** CCPs must comply with the new special resolution regime, including directions from the Bank of England, restrictions on payments, and stabilisation instruments. Clearing members and counterparties must be aware that termination rights may be suspended and that the Bank can transfer business, write down instruments, or appoint temporary managers.
**Where:** CCP rulebooks, clearing member agreements, and internal resolution planning documentation
**Materiality:** medium

## Legal Basis

> "resolution measure" means— (a)the making by the Bank of a stabilisation instrument in relation to a CCP, (b)where an instrument of control under paragraph 38 is in place in relation to a CCP, the exercise by the Bank of any relevant rules of the CCP
