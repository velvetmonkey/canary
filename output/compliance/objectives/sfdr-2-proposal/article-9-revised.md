# [[Article 9]] (revised) — Sustainable category: criteria, exclusions and disclosure obligations

## Obligation

**Who:** Financial market participants manufacturing, managing or making available Sustainable financial products
**What:** Financial products claiming to invest in sustainable companies, assets or activities must qualify under the Sustainable category by meeting defined criteria (e.g. EU PAB replication, Taxonomy-aligned investments ≥15%, EU Green Bonds), apply the broadest set of mandatory exclusions (including fossil fuel value chain, oil/gas expansion, electricity generation >100g CO2e/kWh, plus all Transition and ESG Basics exclusions), identify and disclose principal adverse impacts on sustainability factors, and explain actions taken to address those impacts. At least 70% of investments must comply with the sustainability claim.
**Where:** Pre-contractual disclosures, periodic reports, and websites
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> The sustainable category should consist of financial products that invest in companies, assets or activities that are sustainable or that pursue or positively contribute to environmental and/or social objectives. [...] the exclusions for financial products in this category should extend beyond those for the other two categories and encompass activities where the value chain is associated with fossil fuels, including the expansion of fossil fuels.
