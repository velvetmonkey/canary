# Articles 7 and 9 (revised) — PAI identification and disclosure — Identify and disclose principal adverse impacts for Sustainable and Transition products

## Obligation

**Who:** Financial market participants managing Sustainable or Transition categorised products
**What:** Financial market participants must identify and disclose the principal adverse impacts (PAIs) of their investments on sustainability factors (including climate, water, circular economy, pollution, biodiversity) and explain any actions taken to address those impacts. Voluntary PAI indicators building on Annex I of Delegated Regulation (EU) 2022/1288 and Delegated Regulation (EU) 2023/2772 should be used, with flexibility to use different indicators or qualitative explanations where more appropriate.
**Where:** Pre-contractual disclosures and periodic reports
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> products falling under the sustainable category should identify and disclose the principal adverse impacts of their investments on sustainability factors, and explain any actions taken to address those impacts. [...] financial market participants should have the flexibility to disclose these principal adverse impacts using a different approach, such as different indicators or a qualitative explanation of such impacts and actions, if it fits better the nature of the impact identified or addressed.
