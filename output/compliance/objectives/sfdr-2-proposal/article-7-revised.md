# [[Article 7]] (revised) — Transition category: criteria, exclusions and disclosure obligations

## Obligation

**Who:** Financial market participants manufacturing, managing or making available Transition financial products
**What:** Financial products claiming to support the transition of undertakings or activities towards sustainability must qualify under the Transition category by meeting defined criteria (e.g. EU CTB/PAB tracking, Taxonomy capex-aligned investments, credible transition plans or science-based targets), apply mandatory exclusions (prohibited weapons, tobacco, UNGC/OECD violations, hard coal/lignite, new oil/gas projects), identify and disclose principal adverse impacts, and explain actions taken. At least 70% of investments must comply with the transition claim.
**Where:** Pre-contractual disclosures, periodic reports, and websites
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> The transition category should consist of financial products that aim to support the transition of undertakings, economic activities and other assets towards sustainability, or contribute to such transition. [...] This category should therefore bolster the visibility, transparency and integrity of financial products which invest in the transition of undertakings, economic activities, or other assets towards better environmental or social performance, or that contribute to the environmental or social transition.
