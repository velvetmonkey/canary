# [[Article 9(1)]] (new [[Article 9]] [[SFDR]]) — Sustainable category: 70% threshold and exclusions

## Obligation

**Who:** Financial market participants manufacturing, managing or making available financial products
**What:** To use the 'Sustainable' category label, a product must allocate at least 70% of investments to meet a clear and measurable sustainability objective (environmental or social), apply the full set of exclusions under [[Article 12(1)]] of Delegated Regulation (EU) 2020/1818 plus additional fossil-fuel project exclusions, and identify and disclose principal adverse impacts with actions taken to address them.
**Where:** In pre-contractual disclosures, on websites and in periodic reports
**Deadline:** 18 months after entry into force
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> Financial market participants shall not claim that their financial products invest in sustainable undertakings, sustainable economic activities, or other sustainable assets, or contribute to sustainability, unless those financial products meet the following conditions: (a) they have a 70% threshold linked to the proportion of investments to meet a clear and measurable objective related to sustainability factors
