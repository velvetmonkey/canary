# [[Article 7(1)]] (new [[Article 7]] [[SFDR]]) — Transition category: 70% threshold and exclusions

## Obligation

**Who:** Financial market participants manufacturing, managing or making available financial products
**What:** To use the 'Transition' category label, a product must allocate at least 70% of investments to meet a clear and measurable transition objective, apply specified exclusions (prohibited weapons, tobacco, UNGC/OECD violations, hard coal/lignite, new fossil-fuel projects), and identify and disclose principal adverse impacts with actions taken to address them.
**Where:** In pre-contractual disclosures, on websites and in periodic reports
**Deadline:** 18 months after entry into force
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> Financial market participants shall not claim that their financial products invest in the transition of undertakings, economic activities, or other assets towards sustainability, or contribute to such transition, unless those financial products meet the following conditions: (a) they have a 70% threshold linked to the proportion of investments to meet a clear and measurable transition objective related to sustainability factors
