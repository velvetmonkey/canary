# Articles 7, 8, 9 (revised) — 70% investment threshold — Minimum 70% investment threshold for categorised products

## Obligation

**Who:** Financial market participants manufacturing, managing or making available categorised sustainability-related financial products
**What:** At least 70% of investments in any categorised product (Sustainable, Transition, or ESG Basics) must be made in accordance with the product's sustainability-related claim (i.e. the objective pursued or sustainability considerations applied). The remaining investments must not contradict the sustainability claim. During a disclosed phase-in period, the 70% threshold must be attained at the latest at expiry of that period.
**Where:** Portfolio construction and investment process; disclosed in pre-contractual documents
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> a minimum portion of 70% of investments by financial products in each category should be made in accordance with the sustainability-related claim, i.e. the objective that is pursued or the sustainability-related considerations that are applied. Financial market participants should be allowed to freely allocate the remaining investments based on diversification, hedging or liquidity needs. The se remaining investments should not contradict the sustainability-related claims of the financial product.
