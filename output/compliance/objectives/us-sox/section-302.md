# Section 302 — CEO/CFO Certification of Financial Reports

## Obligation

**Who:** Principal executive officers and principal financial officers of companies filing periodic reports under [[Section 13(a)]] or 15(d) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934
**What:** CEOs and CFOs must personally certify in each annual and quarterly report that: (1) they have reviewed the report; (2) it contains no material misstatements or omissions; (3) financial statements fairly present the financial condition; (4) they are responsible for internal controls; (5) they have disclosed all significant deficiencies and fraud to auditors and the audit committee; and (6) they have indicated any significant changes in internal controls.
**Where:** In each annual or quarterly report filed with the SEC (Forms 10-K, 10-Q)
**Deadline:** Rules effective not later than 30 days after enactment (July 30, 2002)
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> the principal executive officer or officers and the principal financial officer or officers, or persons performing similar functions, certify in each annual or quarterly report filed or submitted under either such section of such Act
