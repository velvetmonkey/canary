# Section 306(a) — Prohibition on Insider Trading During Pension Fund Blackout Periods

## Obligation

**Who:** Directors and executive officers of issuers of equity securities
**What:** It is unlawful for any director or executive officer to purchase, sell, or otherwise acquire or transfer any equity security of the issuer during any pension fund blackout period (a period of more than 3 consecutive business days during which at least 50% of plan participants cannot trade issuer equity in their individual account plans), if the equity was acquired in connection with their service as director or officer. Any profits from violations must be returned to the issuer.
**Where:** Applies to all trading activity by covered persons during blackout periods
**Deadline:** 180 days after enactment
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> it shall be unlawful for any director or executive officer of an issuer of any equity security (other than an exempted security), directly or indirectly, to purchase, sell, or otherwise acquire or transfer any equity security of the issuer (other than an exempted security) during any blackout period
