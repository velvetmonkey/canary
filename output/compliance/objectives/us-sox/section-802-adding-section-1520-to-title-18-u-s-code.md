# Section 802 (adding Section 1520 to Title 18, U.S. Code) — Mandatory Retention of Audit Workpapers

## Obligation

**Who:** Accountants conducting audits of issuers subject to Section 10A(a) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934
**What:** Auditors must retain all audit and review workpapers for a minimum of five years from the end of the fiscal period in which the audit or review was concluded. Knowing and wilful violation is a criminal offence punishable by up to 10 years imprisonment.
**Where:** Auditor's records/files; subject to SEC rules on retention of related documents
**Deadline:** SEC to promulgate rules within 180 days of enactment
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> Any accountant who conducts an audit of an issuer of securities to which section 10A(a) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 (15 U.S.C. 78j-1(a)) applies, shall maintain all audit or review workpapers for a period of 5 years from the end of the fiscal period in which the audit or review was concluded.
