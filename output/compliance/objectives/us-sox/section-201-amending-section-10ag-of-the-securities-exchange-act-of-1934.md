# Section 201 (amending Section 10A(g) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934) — Prohibition on Non-Audit Services to Audit Clients

## Obligation

**Who:** Registered public accounting firms performing audits for issuers
**What:** It is unlawful for an auditor to provide specified non-audit services to an audit client contemporaneously with the audit. Prohibited services include bookkeeping, financial information systems design, appraisal/valuation services, actuarial services, internal audit outsourcing, management functions, broker/dealer/investment advisory services, legal services, and any other service the PCAOB determines impermissible.
**Where:** Applies to the auditor-client relationship; prohibition is absolute for listed services
**Deadline:** 180 days after commencement of PCAOB operations
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> it shall be unlawful for a registered public accounting firm (and any associated person of that firm, to the extent determined appropriate by the Commission) that performs for any issuer any audit required by this title or the rules of the Commission under this title... to provide to that issuer, contemporaneously with the audit, any non-audit service
