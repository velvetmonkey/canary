# Part 1, Chapter 2 — Office for Environmental Protection (Section 22 / Schedule 1) — OEP oversight of public authorities' compliance with environmental law

## Obligation

**Who:** Public authorities (including regulated businesses); the OEP as enforcer
**What:** The OEP has statutory powers to scrutinise, investigate and take enforcement action against public authorities that fail to comply with environmental law. It can issue information notices, decision notices and bring environmental review proceedings in the High Court. Asset managers must be aware that investee companies subject to environmental law face independent OEP scrutiny.
**Where:** OEP enforcement proceedings; High Court (environmental review); Parliament (scrutiny reports)
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> "the court" means—(a)in relation to an environmental review arising under the law of England and Wales or Northern Ireland, the High Court, or (b)in relation to an environmental review arising under the law of Scotland, the Court of Session
