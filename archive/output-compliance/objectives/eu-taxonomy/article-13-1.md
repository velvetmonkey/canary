# Article 13(1) — Substantial contribution criteria for circular economy transition

## Obligation

**Who:** Financial market participants and undertakings assessing taxonomy alignment for circular economy objective
**What:** An economic activity contributes substantially to the circular economy if it achieves one or more of: more efficient use of natural resources; increased product durability/reparability/reusability; increased recyclability; reduced hazardous substances; prolonged product use; increased secondary raw materials; waste prevention; improved waste management infrastructure; minimised incineration/landfilling; or enabling such activities.
**Where:** Applied in investment analysis and taxonomy alignment assessments
**Materiality:** medium

## Legal Basis

> An economic activity shall qualify as contributing substantially to the transition to a circular economy, including waste prevention, re-use and recycling, where that activity: (a) uses natural resources, including sustainably sourced bio-based and other raw materials, in production more efficiently.
