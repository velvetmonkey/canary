# Section 806 (18 U.S.C. § 1514A) — Whistleblower Protection for Employees Reporting Fraud

## Obligation

**Who:** Companies with registered securities or required to file SEC reports; their officers, employees, contractors, subcontractors, and agents
**What:** No covered company or its agents may discharge, demote, suspend, threaten, harass, or otherwise discriminate against an employee who lawfully provides information or assists in an investigation regarding conduct the employee reasonably believes constitutes a violation of federal securities laws or fraud against shareholders. Employees have 90 days to file a complaint with the Secretary of Labor.
**Where:** Applies to all employment actions and conditions
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> No company with a class of securities registered under section 12 of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934... or any officer, employee, contractor, subcontractor, or agent of such company, may discharge, demote, suspend, threaten, harass, or in any other manner discriminate against an employee in the terms and conditions of employment because of any lawful act done by the employee
