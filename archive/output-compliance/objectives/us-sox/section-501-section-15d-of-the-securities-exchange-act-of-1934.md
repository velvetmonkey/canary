# Section 501 (Section 15D of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934) — Analyst Conflict of Interest Disclosure in Research Reports

## Obligation

**Who:** Registered brokers or dealers; securities analysts
**What:** Rules must require each securities analyst to disclose conflicts of interest in public appearances, and each registered broker or dealer to disclose conflicts of interest in each research report, including: analyst debt/equity investments in the subject issuer; compensation received from the issuer; whether the issuer is or has been a client; and whether analyst compensation was based on investment banking revenues.
**Where:** In research reports and public appearances by securities analysts
**Deadline:** 1 year after enactment
**Materiality:** medium

## Legal Basis

> rules reasonably designed to require each securities analyst to disclose in public appearances, and each registered broker or dealer to disclose in each research report, as applicable, conflicts of interest that are known or should have been known by the securities analyst or the broker or dealer
