# Section 306(b) (amending Section 101(i) of ERISA) — Plan Administrator Notice of Blackout Periods to Participants

## Obligation

**Who:** Plan administrators of individual account plans (e.g., 401(k) plans)
**What:** Plan administrators must provide written notice to affected plan participants and beneficiaries at least 30 days in advance of any blackout period, explaining the reasons for the blackout, investments affected, expected beginning date and length, and advising participants to evaluate their investment decisions. The plan administrator must also notify the issuer of any employer securities subject to the blackout.
**Where:** Written notice to plan participants and beneficiaries; notice to the issuer
**Deadline:** 180 days after enactment; initial guidance and model notice by January 1, 2003
**Materiality:** medium

## Legal Basis

> In advance of the commencement of any blackout period with respect to an individual account plan, the plan administrator shall notify the plan participants and beneficiaries who are affected by such action in accordance with this subsection.
