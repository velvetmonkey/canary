# Section 402 (amending Section 13(k) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934) — Prohibition on Personal Loans to Executive Officers and Directors

## Obligation

**Who:** Issuers (as defined in [[Section 2]] of SOX)
**What:** It is unlawful for any issuer, directly or indirectly (including through subsidiaries), to extend or maintain credit, arrange for the extension of credit, or renew an extension of credit in the form of a personal loan to or for any director or executive officer. Pre-existing loans as of enactment are grandfathered unless materially modified.
**Where:** Applies to all issuer financial arrangements with directors and executive officers
**Materiality:** high

## Legal Basis

> It shall be unlawful for any issuer (as defined in section 2 of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002), directly or indirectly, including through any subsidiary, to extend or maintain credit, to arrange for the extension of credit, or to renew an extension of credit, in the form of a personal loan to or for any director or executive officer (or equivalent thereof) of that issuer.
